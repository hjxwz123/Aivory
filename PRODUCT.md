# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Aurelia serves individuals, technical teams, workspace collaborators, and platform administrators who need a self-hosted AI workspace. They use it for sustained chat, document analysis, research, code execution, artifact creation, model operations, and governance rather than one-off prompt demos.

## Product Purpose

Aurelia provides a production-grade, multi-model AI workspace with the control of self-hosting and the workflow depth of leading hosted assistants. Success means users can move between conversation, tools, files, knowledge bases, projects, and team workspaces without losing state or needing to understand the underlying provider infrastructure.

## Brand Personality

Precise, capable, and calm. The product should feel trustworthy under heavy use, technically sophisticated without becoming theatrical, and polished enough that operational details recede behind the user's task.

## Anti-references

- Thin provider wrappers that expose infrastructure limitations to users.
- Marketing-style composition inside authenticated workflows.
- Decorative card grids, gratuitous gradients, oversized controls, and motion without state meaning.
- Interfaces that hide failures, lose work on navigation, or use optimistic local state without a durable recovery path.
- Unfamiliar controls where standard product affordances are clearer.

## Design Principles

- Preserve work across refreshes, reconnects, retries, and provider failures.
- Make long-running AI and document operations observable, actionable, and honest.
- Keep the interface quiet and familiar while supporting expert-level depth.
- Treat server state as authoritative and reconcile local optimism explicitly.
- Keep security, ownership, billing, and workspace boundaries visible in behavior without adding friction to routine work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard operability across the product. Respect reduced-motion preferences, maintain visible focus states, avoid color-only status communication, support light and dark themes, and keep all user-facing workflows available in the product's five supported languages.
